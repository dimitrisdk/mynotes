# Apple WWDC 2026: Gemini-Powered Siri Overview

**Date:** June 8, 2026  
**Source:** BuildFastWithAI blog post "AI News Today - June 7, 2026"  
**Tags:** #ai-news #apple #wwdc2026 #siri #gemini #google

## Key Points

- Apple WWDC 2026 opens June 8, 2026 at 10 a.m. PT (Tim Cook's final keynote).
- Siri is being overhauled to run on a custom 1.2-trillion-parameter Google Gemini model.
- The Gemini model is licensed from Google at approximately $1 billion per year.
- This is the largest commercial deployment of Gemini outside Google.
- Apple introduces a multi-model Extensions system allowing users to choose between ChatGPT, Gemini, or Claude for Apple Intelligence, each with a distinct voice (Gemini as default).
- Other expected releases: iOS 27, macOS 27, iPadOS/watchOS/tvOS/visionOS updates; AI-powered Photos editing (background extension, reframing); possible tease of an M5-based HomePad smart-home hub.
- Strategic implication: Apple's decision not to build its own frontier model is a contrarian bet focusing on private compute, hardware integration, and multi-provider flexibility.

## Significance for Dimitris

- Demonstrates the viability of licensing frontier models for integrated AI experiences.
- Highlights the growing importance of multi-model strategies in consumer AI.
- May influence decisions about AI agent architecture for personal use (e.g., using multiple models via Hermes Agent).
- The $1B/year licensing cost underscores the expense of frontier models, reinforcing the value of local/open-source alternatives like those supported by Hermes Agent.
